Hyperlive

Cartograms are maps where the shapes of countries or regions are distorted to reflect datasets such as population or income. We created the first interactive animated version, which we named ‘squishy maps’.

The first squishy map was the Carbon Map. The next was Better or Worse. We're currently working on a tool to enable anyone to make their own squishy maps.

How it works

Our cartogram tool is based on this publicly available algorithm by Mark Newman (which works, ingeniously, by simulating the diffusion of chemicals between areas, as described in this paper by Newman and Michael Gastner). They're generated in advance as JSON files rather than created on the fly in the browser.
