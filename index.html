Recent projects

The Carbon Map — an interactive website using a newly developed animated cartogram tool (aka "squishy maps") to explore responsibility for climate change. It was featured prominently by the Guardian and was a finalist in the World Bank’s Apps for Climate competition.

Better or Worse — a new kind of interactive animation (a "Torkie") which summarise twenty years of global economic and environmental data to ask whether the world is getting better or worse. Created for the Guardian Global Development Desk.

Where they started — a fun interactive squishy map created for the charity Join In to show which British counties have produced which Olympians in which sports since the modern games began in 1896.

People

Kiln was founded and is run by Robin Houston and Duncan Clark but draws on a network of others on a project-by-project basis.

Duncan Clark has worked in journalism, books and campaigning as a writer, editor and strategist. He’s a consultant environment editor at the Guardian and previously worked at Profile Books, 10:10, BBC Worldwide and elsewhere.

Robin Houston is a web developer and mathematician. He works as a developer at MySociety, before which he was based at the Sanger Institute, 10:10 and the Guardian, where he built the original website. Robin has a PhD in category theory.

FAQ

Why ‘Kiln’?

Information is like clay: it exists in abundance but only becomes interesting when it's been processed, shaped and glazed. A kiln is a furnace where materials and form come together to create something useful or beautiful.

So Kiln doesn’t stand for anything?

Not really. We could say ‘Knowledge, Ideas, Learning, News’, but that would be a backronym.

Are you Italian?

No. We chose the .it domain because kiln is also a verb (‘to kiln it’) and also because it’s a homophone for ‘killin’ it’ (which, among other things, can mean ‘to be doing something very good’).
