Recent projects↑

The Carbon Map

An interactive website using our ‘squishy maps’ (cartograms) to explore responsibility for climate change. It was featured prominently by the Guardian and was a finalist in the World Bank’s Apps for Climate competition.

Better or Worse

An interactive animation (‘Talkie’) which summarise twenty years of global economic and environmental data to ask whether the world is getting better or worse. Created for the Guardian Global Development Desk.

Where they started

A fun interactive squishy map created for the charity Join In to show which British counties have produced which Olympians in which sports since the modern games began in 1896.

FAQ↑

Why ‘Kiln’?

Information is like clay: it exists in abundance but only becomes interesting when it’s been processed, shaped and glazed. A kiln is a furnace where materials and form come together to create something useful or beautiful.

So Kiln doesn’t stand for anything?

Not really. We could say ‘Knowledge, Ideas, Learning, News’, but that would be a backronym.

Are you Italian?

No. We chose the .it domain because kiln is also a verb (‘to kiln it’) and also because it’s a homophone for ‘killin’ it’ (which, among other things, can mean ‘to be doing something very good’).
