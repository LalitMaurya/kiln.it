Projects

We launched in March 2012 with CarbonMap.org, a website using a newly developed animated cartogram tool to explore responsibility for climate change. It was featured prominently by the Guardian but was created as a submission for the World Bank’s Apps for Climate competition.

An interactive animation to mark the Rio+20 conference. The interactive summarises 20 years of global economic and environmental data to ask whether the world is getting better or worse. Users can give their view and see which genders, age groups and continents are most positive or negative. It was created for the Guardian Global Development Desk.

A fun interactive map created for Join In to show which counties have produced which Olympians. The map uses an animated cartogram to show trends both expected (boxers tend to come from big cities, and sailors from the coast) and unexpected (South Wales's gymnastic eminence or the North West's skill at swimming).

We’re also developing a number of other projects in association with various partners and clients. If you’re interested in working with us, get in touch.

FAQ

• Why ‘Kiln’?

Information is a bit like clay: it exists in abundance but only becomes interesting when it finds the right form. A kiln is a furnace where that form – a combination of materials, craft and design – is turned into something useful or beautiful.

• So Kiln doesn’t stand for anything?

Not really. We could say ‘Knowledge, Ideas, Learning, News’, but that would be a backronym.

• Are you Italian?

No. We chose the .it domain because kiln is also a verb (‘to kiln it’) and also because it’s a homophone for ‘killin’ it’ (which, among other things, can mean ‘to be doing something very good’).
