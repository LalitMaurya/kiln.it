Talkies

Most interactives expect the viewer to do all the work. As a result, many are confusing and unengaging. Conventional videos and animations are simpler and better at telling a story, but they're not interactive.

We created ‘Talkies’ to combine the best elements of both formats by adding voiceovers and animations to interactives â€“ just as talkies once added sound to silent movies.

The first Talkie was the Carbon Map. The next was Where They Started. We're currently working on a tool to enable anyone to make their own squishy maps without any technical know-how.

How it works

Layman—Talkies use timecodes associated with a voiceover audio file to trigger actions in the interactive, such as sliding between panels, ‘clicking’ buttons, animating graphs or drawing shapes.

Technical—Talkies use the HTML5 multimedia layer to trigger events, such as graphs drawn and animated in SVG. A typical talkie will work on modern browsers and IE9 and above, though with sufficient effort and time it's possible create Flash back-ups for older browsers.
