    Characters

    Shame we cannot have a map to scale. I'm fairly certain the UK isn't anywhere near the illustrated size compared to say France.

    Having a map to scale is not as simple as you make out, given that the Earth is, well, a sphere, and maps are flat. As the Guardian itself says:

    Showing a spherical planet on a flat piece of paper has troubled cartographers for centuries. We have used the Robinson projection, developed by Arthur Robinson in 1961 as a way of showing the entire world at once . The Mercator projection - first developed in 1569 - is still used by many online mapping services, such as Google, but it does not represent the earth well on paper. You would not be able to use this map to navigate across the planet but it still represents
    one of the clearest ways of illustrating the globe

    There are equal-area projections, eg http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gall%E2%80%93Peters_projection but they often have their own problems, such as distorting the angles and distances between points.

    There's no such thing as a perfect map projection - what would be your preferred alternative? What are its shortcomings?
